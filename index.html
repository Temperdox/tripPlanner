📅 Trip Itinerary (Winter 2025)

Dec 1-2

Tokyo Arrival & Winter Exploration

Land at Narita/Haneda, check into Henn na Hotel Maihama. Explore Tokyo Disney area winter illuminations and nearby Ikspiari shopping mall.

Dec 3-4

Central Tokyo Winter Adventures

Shibuya Sky winter views, Harajuku, Meiji Shrine. Visit Don Quijote Shibuya store. Explore Akihabara electronics district and Yodobashi Akiba. Winter illuminations at night.

Dec 5

Traditional Tokyo

Visit Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa, traditional shopping street. Explore traditional Japanese culture and winter seasonal decorations.

Dec 6-7

Cat Island Adventure (Weather Permitting)

Travel to Aoshima "Cat Island" - check ferry schedules for winter operations. May need backup indoor cat cafe activities if ferries cancelled.

Dec 8-9

Kyoto Winter Cultural Immersion

Fushimi Inari Shrine in winter, Kiyomizu-dera Temple, Gion District. Traditional kaiseki dinner and possible geisha spotting. Winter bamboo groves.

Dec 10

Nara Winter Day Trip

Visit Nara Park, feed the deer in winter setting, explore Todaiji Temple with the Great Buddha, and Kasuga Taisha Shrine with winter lanterns.

Dec 11-12

Osaka Food & Winter Fun

Dotonbori district winter illuminations, Universal Studios Japan holiday events, Osaka Castle. Famous takoyaki and okonomiyaki, plus warming winter comfort foods.

Dec 13

Mount Fuji Winter Views & Hot Springs

Hakone region, Lake Kawaguchi for snow-capped Fuji views, onsen experience perfect for winter. No climbing season but excellent viewing weather.

Dec 14

Final Tokyo Shopping & Departure

Last-minute shopping at Don Quijote, souvenir hunting in Asakusa, winter seasonal items, departure preparations.

Week 1 (Dec 1-7)

Tokyo Deep Winter Dive

Comprehensive Tokyo exploration: all major districts with winter illuminations. Shibuya, Harajuku, Akihabara, Asakusa, Ginza, Roppongi. Multiple Don Quijote visits and Yodobashi Akiba. Christmas markets and winter events.

Week 2 (Dec 8-14)

Kansai Region Winter Adventure

Extended time in Kyoto (winter temples, bamboo forest in snow, traditional districts), Osaka (winter food culture, USJ holiday events), Nara Park winter deer interactions and illuminated temples.

Week 3 (Dec 15-21)

Hidden Winter Gems & Nature

Cat islands (weather permitting), Mount Fuji region snow views, Hakone hot springs, Nikko winter temples and snow monkeys, lesser-known winter destinations and seasonal experiences.

Week 4 (Dec 22-28)

Christmas & Year-End Celebrations

Experience Japanese Christmas culture, winter illuminations peak season, choose from: Hiroshima winter Peace Park, Kanazawa snow country, Takayama winter festivals, or northern winter regions.

Final Days (Dec 29-30)

Tokyo New Year Preparation

Return to Tokyo for New Year preparations, final shopping, revisiting favorite winter spots, year-end temple visits, departure preparations.

💡 Essential Winter Travel Tips

🎫 Transportation (Winter)

JR Pass prices increased 70% in 2023 - calculate if individual tickets are cheaper. IC cards work great for local transport. Some rural ferries have limited winter service.

❄️ Winter Weather

Pack layers! Tokyo: 2-12°C (35-54°F). Snow possible but not heavy. Indoor heating can be intense. Bring warm socks for temple visits (shoe removal required).

💴 Money Matters

Japan still largely cash-based. Withdraw from 7-Eleven ATMs. Winter heating costs may increase some prices. Budget ¥12,000-18,000 cash per day in winter.

🍜 Winter Food Culture

Perfect season for hot ramen, oden (hot pot), takoyaki, and warming comfort foods. Convenience stores offer amazing hot foods. Try seasonal winter flavors!

🎌 Winter Cultural Experiences

December illuminations are spectacular. New Year preparations start early. Hot springs (onsen) are perfect for cold weather. Winter temple visits are serene.

🛍️ Winter Shopping Strategy

Winter sales season! Tax-free shopping available. Don Quijote for winter items and heating goods. Department stores have amazing winter food sections. Holiday-themed goods available.
